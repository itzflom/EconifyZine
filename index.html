About the Zine
"We value critical thinking over certainty and clarity over complexity."

Econify Zine is a student-led youth publication dedicated to making economics more accessible, relevant, and engaging. We bridge the gap between academic economic theory and the real-world issues that shape our lives.

We publish student-written articles, commentaries, and analyses on current economic, political, and social developments. Through creative storytelling and critical thinking, we amplify youth perspectives in global economic discussions.

Our goal is not to provide definitive answers, but to equip young people with the tools and confidence to think for themselves — and to question the systems around them without fear.

Founder's Note
"Power is never free. Nations pay for it with the strength of their institutions. Markets pay for it with uncertainty. Individuals pay for it with trust — the most fragile currency of all."

There's a moment, quiet, almost imperceptible, when the world shifts. When you realise that so much of what we call power isn't natural or inevitable, but something far more fragile: a collective agreement. A shared illusion. An arrangement we accept because everyone else seems to accept it too.

Econify Zine was born from this moment of questioning — from the desire to understand the real cost of the systems we live in. Our first edition, The Price of Power, is an invitation to look beyond the surface, to trace the hidden lines between influence and inequality, between ambition and accountability.

Power behaves like a shadow. It looks tangible, stretches across entire nations, but its shape depends entirely on the light behind it. Change the angle, shift the source, and the shadow transforms. What once seemed stable becomes fragile.

Enjoy this issue, and keep questioning.

Alessa Efridya Ningtyas Founder & Chief Editor

Prof. Bhimani's research deals with financial controls and management, AI and digitilisation aspects of globalisation, governance and economic growth. He sits on university advisory boards across Europe, Africa, America and Asia. He is a Canadian CPA with an MBA from Cornell University and a PhD from LSE.
